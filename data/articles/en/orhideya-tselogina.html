Care for the Cattleya Orchid

How to properly transplant the Cattleya Orchid

The Cattleya orchid should be transplanted every 2â€“3 years or when the substrate becomes depleted. It is best done after flowering.

The substrate should be breathable, consisting of bark, charcoal, and sphagnum. Drainage at the bottom of the pot is essential.

After transplanting, the plant should be placed in an area with diffused light to help it adapt.

When to water the Cattleya Orchid

The Cattleya orchid needs regular watering. Water the plant as soon as the substrate dries out, avoiding complete drying of the substrate.

Use soft, warm water. It is important to avoid water stagnation in the roots.

Fertilizing the Cattleya Orchid

Fertilize during the active growth period every two weeks. Use a weak solution of orchid fertilizers.

Changes in the Cattleya Orchid

Spots on leaves or wilting may be related to insufficient light or improper watering.

Additional Information about the Cattleya Orchid

The Cattleya Orchid is known for its exquisite flowers and fragrance. It prefers bright, but diffused light and high humidity.
