Care for Violet Plant

How to Properly Transplant a Violet

Violets, or Saintpaulias, require transplantation as they grow. Here are the main points to consider:

Transplanting is necessary if the violet has stopped growing, there is a white residue on the soil surface, the lower part of the plant is exposed, or the roots have outgrown the pot.

Transplanting Methods:

1. Transfer: Moving to a larger pot without removing the old soil.

2. Complete soil replacement: Full soil change, especially in case of root diseases.

3. Partial soil replacement: Partial soil replacement for young plants.

Pot selection: Violets prefer small pots. For large plants, a 12 cm pot is sufficient, and for young plants, 5 cm is enough.

When to Water a Violet

Summer: Water violets every 2-3 days using warm (22-24°C) settled water.

Winter: Reduce watering to once a week, avoiding overwatering.

Watering Methods:

1. Regular: Watering with a watering can with a small stream.

2. Tray method: Place the pot in a tray of water for 35 minutes.

3. Drip method: Watering with a thin stream, avoiding leaves.

4. Immersion: Soaking the pot in a basin of water for an hour.

5. Wick method: Using a wick for constant moisture supply.

Water Spraying for Violets

Violets have specific requirements for water spraying:

African violets should NOT be sprayed directly on the leaves as water droplets can cause brown spots and damage the delicate leaf structure. Instead, you can increase humidity around the plant by:

1. Using a humidity tray: Place the pot on a tray filled with pebbles and water, ensuring the pot doesn't sit directly in water.

2. Room humidifier: Use a humidifier near your violets, especially during dry winter months.

3. Grouping plants: Place violets near other houseplants to create a microclimate with higher humidity.

If you must spray, do so very carefully around the plant but not directly on the foliage, and only use room temperature water. The recommended interval between humidity maintenance is about 5 days.

Fertilization for Violets

To maintain health and flowering of violets, use the following fertilizers:

Nitrogen fertilizers: For young plants, promoting leaf growth.

Potassium and phosphorus fertilizers: For flowering violets, improving blooming.

Complex fertilizers: Liquid fertilizers for decorative plants containing all necessary elements.

Fertilization frequency: Fertilize violets every 15 days, combining with watering.

External Changes of Violets

Violets can face various problems:

Diseases:

Powdery mildew: White coating on stems, treated with benlate.

Gray rot: Gray fluffy coating, treated with Fitosporin, Thiram, Agat.

Fusarium: Root rot, treated with benomyl or fundazol.

Pests:

Mites: Red spots on leaves, treated with Actellic.

Aphids: Infest buds, treated with Actellic or Mospilan.

Mealybugs: Red spots on leaves, treated with Fitoverm or Aktara.

Additional Information About Violets

Pruning: Regularly trim excess leaves to maintain the rosette shape.

Hygiene: Remove faded flowers and diseased leaves.

Lighting: Violets prefer diffused light, avoid direct sunlight.

Temperature: Optimal temperature is 20-25°C in summer and 20°C in winter.
