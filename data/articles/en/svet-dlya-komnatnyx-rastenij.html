Light for Houseplants

Sunlight plays a very important role in the development of plants, but different plants prefer different light intensities. Some don't mind basking in direct sunlight, while others prefer shade. Some plants love diffused light, while others are fine with just a regular light bulb.

This article lists the main, widely popular houseplants and their light preferences.

Plants that Prefer Shade

Aspidistra, aglaonema, gloxinia, sansevieria (mother-in-law's tongue or snake plant). As can be seen from the list, there are not many such plants, but this doesn't mean they should be placed in dark corners. They feel quite well in partial shade.

Plants that Thrive in Partial Shade

Dracaena fragrans, dracaena marginata, common ivy, tolmia, fatsia, scindapsus, ferns, dwarf ficus, fittonia, creeping philodendron, fatshedera, as well as those that prefer shade.

Plants that Do Well in Diffused Sunlight

Asparagus, anthurium, azalea, royal begonia, grapevine, bromeliads, dieffenbachia, dysygotena, Christmas cactus (zigocactus), monstera, oxalis, columnea, peperomia, lily, scindapsus, philodendron, peace lily, fuchsia, chlorophytum, muraya, schefflera, cyclamen. Most decorative foliage and flowering plants prefer such conditions. One of the brightest places in the apartment is the windowsill, although the area nearby is also fine. However, it is important to remember that at half a meter from the windowsill, the amount of light is halved.

Plants that Can Tolerate Direct Sunlight

Gynura, beloperone, zebrina, gloxinia, croton, capsicum, cordyline terminalis, cuphea, nertera, mimosa, sansevieria, poinsettia, streptocarpus, senecio, tradescantia, chlorophytum, hoya, chrysanthemum, rubber plant.

Of course, these are not all the plants, but only those that are most commonly found on windowsills or in apartments, both by amateur gardeners and housewives. Sunlight for plants is as necessary as air and water, but in moderate amounts. This condition is essential for the normal development of each plant species. At the same time, it is important to strictly follow the care instructions for plants and create suitable conditions for their normal growth, including providing the required amount of light. A plant can feel poorly both from a lack of light and from an excess, and the effects of direct sunlight can cause burns. In this case, the result can be disastrous, and starting over is always difficult.
